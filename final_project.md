# Final Project – Quantum Network Use Case Pitch

## Assignment

Throughout this course, we have learned that a quantum network enables a range of applications, from superdense coding and quantum money to anonymous transmission and beyond. 
The field is young, and many applications remain unexplored. This is your chance to be creative and entrepreneurial! 
**Your challenge is to pick a quantum network application (from the course, the literature or your own) and build a new use case or business idea around it.**
You should implement it using the SimulaQron SDK and the tools you have learned during the course, and present a short business pitch of your project.

## Example

To illustrate, here is an example of the thought process:

1. Quantum network application: CHSH game
2. What this enables: Distributed nodes can coordinate their decisions without latency
3. Use case: Load balancing in classical network

This example is based on "Entanglement improves coordination in distributed systems" by F. Ferreira da Silva and S. Wehner (arXiv:2602.04588).
Your goal is to come up with your own idea following a similar line of reasoning.

## Deliverables

Your group will produce two deliverables:

1. A 5-minute pitch: Present your idea as a convincing pitch. Clearly explain the real-world problem you are solving, which quantum network application you are building on, where the quantum advantage comes from, and analyse the security and/or performance of your approach.
2. A software demo: A working implementation using the SimulaQron SDK that demonstrates the quantum protocol in action within the context of your use case. 

One person per group can hand in both through the Assignments page one Brightspace. Please include a text file listing all members of the group.

## Grading

The minimum expectation is a working and correct implementation of your application paired with a business idea.
The ceiling is a compelling, well-argued business pitch with a polished demo that convinces us to invest in it.

Your project will be evaluated on the following criteria:

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Novelty & Creativity** | How original is the application? Does it go beyond a straightforward textbook protocol? |
| **Protocol Correctness** | Is the underlying quantum protocol used correctly? Does the demo actually work? |
| **Code Quality** | Is the code clean, readable, documented and well-structured? |
| **Pitch Quality** | Is the presentation clear, convincing, and well-delivered? |
